Welcome,

Andrzej Piotr Kądzielawa (Ostrava, Czechia) is a theoretical physicist and HPC specialist.

He earned his PhD in Physics in Prof. Józef Spałek's group at Jagiellonian University (Kraków, Poland) in 2015 with summa cum laude honors following his 4-year work on his original quantum chemical method applied in the theoretical condensed matter physics. This included high-performance computations on the elecronic structure of solid hydrogen, completed using an original open-sourced library qmt created together with Dr. Andrzej Biborski.

Since 2011 he workes and teaches at Faculty of Physics, Astronomy and Applied Computer Science at Jagiellonian University (cf. Teaching section). He coordinated acquisition of computational cluster for Department of Condensed Matter Theory, which he administers ever since. In September 2018 he has also got a position as Reasercher in the National Supercomputing Centre IT4Innovations at the Technical University of Ostrava (Czech Republic).
